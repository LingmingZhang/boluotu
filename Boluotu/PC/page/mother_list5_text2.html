听国外宝妈达人教你如何让

Toddlers are just like the rest of us – they don't always listen. At their age, they need you to teach them how to pay attention. "But what often happens," says Roni Leiderman, associate dean of the Family Center at Nova Southeastern University in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, "is that parents say something 10 times, then they start counting down to punishment."
孩子就像我们其余人一样----他们并不总是倾听。在这个年龄，他们需要你教他如何集中注意力。在佛罗里达州劳德代尔堡诺瓦东南大学家庭中心副院长罗尼.雷德曼说“但是经常发生的是，父母说了一件事十遍以上，孩子不听，他们就会对孩子有惩罚措施。”
That doesn't teach a child to listen – it teaches him that he can ignore you the first 10 times you say something and only pay attention when you're about to lose it. Getting your child to listen doesn't have to be a battle. Here are some tips to make it easier.
这不是在教孩子倾听，这让孩子觉得她可以忽略你前十次说些什么，只注意你什么时候错过。让你的孩子学会倾听不需要像打仗一样，这里有些建议，让这件事变得更容易
Get on his level
和他处于同个平面
As every parent realizes sooner or later, bellowing from a great height (much less from the other room) rarely has the desired effect. Squat down or pick up your child, so you can look him in the eye and get his attention.
每个家长迟早会意识到，站着对你孩子大声说话很少有预期的效果（在另一个房间会更少）。蹲着或接近你的孩子，你能看着他的眼睛，引起他的注意。
Eye contact is important and works best when you're face to face with your child. He'll listen much more closely if you sit down next to him at the breakfast table when reminding him to eat up his cornflakes, or perch on his bed at night when telling him you're about to turn out the light.
当你和孩子面对面交流时，眼神交流很重要，效果也最好。在餐桌上，如果你坐在他旁边提醒他吃玉米片，他会听的更密切，或者晚上在他床边告诉他你要关灯。
Be clear
叙述明确
State your message clearly, simply, and with quiet authority. Your child will zone out if you harp on a topic too long. It's hard to find the point of a wordy message such as "It's really cold outside, and you've been sick lately, so I want you to put on your sweater before we go to the store."
清楚、简单、带点威严的陈述你的信息。你喋喋不休的讲一个话题，你的孩子会开小差。孩子很难在一句冗长的信息里找到重点，比如“外面很冷，你最近一直在生病，所以在去商店之前，我想让你穿上你的毛衣。”
On the other hand, "It's time to get your sweater" is unmistakable. And don't phrase something as a question if your child doesn't actually have a choice. "It's time to climb into your car seat" has a lot more impact than "Come climb into your car seat, okay, sweetie?"
在另一方面，确凿无疑的是“是时候把你的毛衣穿上”。不要用类似问题的语句描述你的信息，你的孩子实际上并不能有选择。“你该坐进你的车座位”比“坐进你的车座位好吗，宝贝？”效果更好。
It's good to give toddlers choices, but be sure you're okay with all the options you offer – and stick with only two. By allowing your toddler to make limited choices, she'll feel empowered (and you'll be satisfied with the result).
给孩子一些选择也是可行的，但你要确保给他的选择都是可行的，坚持只有两个选项。允许他做出的有限选择，他会感受到获得自己的权利（你对结果也会感到满意）
